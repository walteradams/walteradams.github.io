Delivering Client Expectations

In our initial meetings the client and I developed a persona by examining competitors and other similar websites in different marketplaces. We decided upon a persona that represented a night out; dark but with vibrant colours, and images of people enjoying themselves — demonstrating a lifestyle the app can help to achieve.

When discussing a design project with a client, I find it beneficial to determine keywords and keyphrases the client often uses in describing their project. I’ll make notes of these keyphrases without making the client aware of them, then slowly feed them back into the discussion to judge the client’s reaction. Doing this results in determining exactly what the client would like and filters out any ideas they may have mentioned but perhaps weren’t so sure about. This was additionally beneficial for the Social Axis project as the keyphrases I had identified assisted us in creating some of the copy for the website as well.

Designing a Website to Reflect an Exclusive Lifestyle

We decided to give the landing page the most design attention as this would be the page that best explains the app to potential users and lead them into the sign up process.

In the header section, we aimed to tell the whole story of the app; describing what it does, how it benefits the user, show the lifestyle other users have, and then prompt the user to sign in via their Facebook account.

The title in the header describes what the app does and is paired with the sign up call to action button. To make signing-up more alluring, we described one of the benefits underneath the call to action. The content of the header section is laid out to create a V shape going from the navigation, along the sides of the title, meeting in the middle at the call to action button — this shape helps draw the user’s eye toward the call to action (the next step in the process we want to take them to).

The header’s background image shows the lifestyle of other Social Axis users — a happy group of people enjoying a night out. I carefully chose this image knowing that psychologically, people tend to look where other people are looking. Although the group of people in the image are looking down at a phone (demonstrating their use of the Social Axis app), they’re also looking at the website’s call to action button. This goes nicely with the V shape created by the layout of the content elements, again, helping draw the user’s eye to the call to action button. 

If at this point the design of the header hasn’t quite done enough to convince the user to sign up, we use the rest of the landing page to expand upon what has already been presented. 

The carousel below the header shows the lifestyle again; people enjoying their exclusive access and benefits from using the app. We then show the process of signing up, booking a table, and getting rewarded — which also describe more benefits to the user.

Having shown more of the lifestyle and benefits, the footer uses another call to action button to tempt the user into signing in. The footer’s background image — like the header — shows people having fun but this time they are further into the night, no longer sitting in a group but now on the dance floor. The progression from the top of the page to the bottom follows the linearity of a night out, suggesting to the user this fun and exclusive lifestyle is going on right now, which they could be part of courtesy of the app. It also creates a connection with the call to action button and can be seen as people reaching up to attain access to this cool lifestyle, making signing up more alluring and reinstilling its exclusivity.

Aside from the landing page, I also designed other pages such as About, Contact, My Profile, nightclub descriptions and the checkout process. These mockups were provided to the client in mobile and desktop versions, which I later coded into the actual website and app.

The logo was designed by a third-party. The majority of the icons were sourced from thenounproject.com.

Building a Fast and Responsive Front-End

The client was very happy with the designs and with the back-end already in place we were able to quickly move onto the build of the front-end.

As the website also acts as the app — which at the time of writing is to be made into a native app using a technology such as PhoneGap — it was important to make sure the front-end had a robust layout that performed well on mobile, tablet, and desktop devices.

I built the front-end “mobile-up”, meaning it was first created for mobile layouts, then using media queries and progressive enhancement additional styles and functionality were added for tablet and desktop.

To keep performance optimal, I used hardware acceleration for smooth animations across all devices, minimized and concatenated scripts, and used sprite sheets to keep the number of browser requests at a minimum.

Where possible, I relied on third-party libraries such as:

- jQuery for general behaviours
- jQuery UI for UI elements such as prettier dropdowns, datepickers etc
- Slick a responsive carousel used on the home and club description pages
- Modernizr for testing browser features
- Selectivizr and Respond for support in older versions of Internet Explorer

The front end build process included the use of Grunt for automated tasks to speed up development and Git for version control.

Conclusion

With my wide range of skills I was able to deliver the design and front-end development tasks the client came to me for, but also helped in areas that perhaps the client wasn’t expecting. My technique of determining key words and phrases in our initial meetings helped construct the content and copy for the website, and I was also able to assist in some areas of the back-end as well.

As a company in the start-up phase, I believe Social Axis found great value in my wide skill set, applying myself and providing advise in design, development, and user experience. Having one “go-to” guy made the process simple and affordable, resulting in an application that looks great, targets maximum number of new user sign ups, and is easy and fast to use.

I had a great time working on Social Axis and look forward to working with them again as the company and application progresses.
